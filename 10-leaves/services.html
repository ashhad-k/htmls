Regulatory License &
Authorisations

 
 

Entities that conduct financial services from the ADGM require authorizations from the Financial Services Regulatory Authority (FSRA). Like any other onshore financial centre, the ADGM has a well structured road to authorisation.

The process is detailed and requires a focused approach, comprehensive documentation and navigation through a host of rules and regulations.

Financial services firms must demonstrate they have the systems and controls needed to manage regulatory risk, and checks and balances that are crucial for operating a financial services business.

Compliance Consulting

 
 

Pre-authorisation

The FSRA focusses on compliance as being a key area of consideration during the process of evaluating an application for financial services. It mandates the appointment of a UAE-resident Compliance Officer (CO) and a Money Laundering Reporting Officer (MLRO), and the creation of a robust compliance department at the firm.

IRAP & ICAAP

 
 

The Internal Risk Assessment Process (IRAP) and Internal Capital Adequacy Assessment Process (ICAAP) are key documents that may be required as part of the regulatory application process.

The level of detail in the IRAP and ICAAP documents will vary based on the size and complexity of the applicant firm, and also on the financial services that have been applied for. Details on structure and governance, risk appetite, capital and liquidity planning, and stress testing are typically included in these documents.

Special Purpose Vehicles

 
 

ADGM Special Purpose Vehicles are flexible, robust, simple and efficient structures designed to suit a variety of requirements. ADGM SPVs can be used to hold assets, create joint ventures and ring-fence assets and liabilities. The ease and cost-effectiveness of the setup, coupled with straightforward ongoing reporting requirements and the common law framework make these SPVs the vehicles of choice in the GCC.

 

Key advantages

  • Low cost
  • No restrictions on nationality of ownership
  • Can hold real property in Dubai and the U.A.E.
  • No restrictions on number of shareholders

  • Corporate directors permitted
  • No minimum share capital
  • Different classes of shares possible

  • Common Law jurisdiction

Startups in ADGM

 
 

A first in the region, ADGM has a quantified approach towards startups and boutique firms. It recognizes the ability for newer players to enter the market is key to developing a broad-based and thriving financial centre. With this in mind, the ADGM framework offers many incentives for startup firms to set up base and obtain authorizations to do business.

 

Activity-based fee structure

 
 

Unlike other regional financial centres, which implement a tier (or category) based fee structure, the ADGM has opted for an activity-based fee structure. Fees for FSRA authorizations start from as low as US$ 5,000 for a single activity of advising on investments. This helps startups to keep costs down at the onset, and add activities as and when opportunities arise.
