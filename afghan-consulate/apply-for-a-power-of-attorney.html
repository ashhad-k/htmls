Application Requirements for Power of Attorney

Presence
  • The applicant(s) and all the witnesses should be present at the Consulate General with a completed application form and required documents.\

The following persons and documents MUST be present at the Consulate General during your appointment.

Application Form
  • The client should carefully complete the application form in Dari or Pashto, paying special attention to names, dates, figures, and places. The application should also clearly mention the subject of the Power of Attorney and the limits of the Attorney's authority.
Required documents and witnesses

The following persons and documents should be available in the Consulate General:

  • Power of Attorney for Marriage Certificate:
    • One of the spouses (husband or wife) who lives in the UAE should visit the Consulate General with: - A request letter outlining the services required and providing information on the date and place of marriage; - A copy of Tazkira or valid Afghan passport and two recent photos (size: 3x4 cm) of both spouses.
      1. Two witnesses (adult & male) with their copies of Tazkira or Afghan passport and two recent photos (size: 3x4 cm);
      2. Copy of Tazkira and photo of the attorney/legal representative in Afghanistan.
      3. All other Types of Power of Attorney:
      4. All the client/s with copies of their Tazkira or Afghan passports with two recent photos (size: 3x4 cm);
      5. Two witnesses with copies of their Tazkira or Afghan passport and two recent photos (size: 3x4 cm);
      6. A copy of Tazkira or a passport and two photos of the legal representative in Afghanistan or any other country are required.
      7. In property-related cases, if the property is inherited, a copy of the Certificate of Inheritance and a copy of the proof of property’s ownership (Sale Deed/ Qabala) or its details are needed.
Fee
  • An applicant should pay 125 USD for one power of attorney.
  • Attestation of a power of attorney will be free of charge if issued in Afghanistan and attested by the Ministry of Foreign Affairs. It will be charged 125 USD if issued in other countries.

Frequently asked questions

Our dedicated support team is available around the clock to assist you with any queries or technical issues you might encounter.

The General Consulate of Afghanistan provides consular services, supports Afghan nationals, and promotes diplomatic and economic relations with the host country. It issues passports and visas, assists in emergencies, and facilitates trade and investment. Operating under the Afghan Embassy, it reports to Afghanistan's Ministry of Foreign Affairs.

To apply, complete the application form available on our website. Submit it along with required documents, such as a valid passport, photos, and fees, either in person or by mail. We will process your application and notify you of the outcome via email or phone.

The processing time typically ranges from 5 to 10 business days, depending on the service requested and the completeness of your application. Expedited services may be available for an additional fee.

The consulate is open Monday to Friday, from 9:00 AM to 3:00 PM, except on public holidays. For specific services, it's recommended to schedule an appointment in advance.
