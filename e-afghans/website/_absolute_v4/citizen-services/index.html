New Passport Application

Download Guide Apply for New Passport

Please read the information below carefully and make sure you are eligible before submitting an application.

Important:

For applicants aged 18 and above, an existing passport is required; otherwise, the application will be rejected. For newborns and dependents under 18, applications are accepted without an existing passport.

Eligibility for New Passport Application

Before proceeding with the new passport application, please check if you are eligible:

  • You are an Afghan citizen (proof of nationality required).
  • You have an existing passport (applications without one will be rejected).
  • You provide your full and correct personal details in the application.
  • You give full consent to the terms and conditions of the application.
  • You are willing to go through a basic identity check (KYC verification) to confirm your details and prevent fraud.
  • You do not have any legal restrictions, pending cases, or travel bans that may prevent passport issuance.
  • You are submitting all mandatory supporting documents (such as Tazkira, residency proof, photographs, and fees).

For newborns and dependents under 18 years old, the following apply:

  • An existing passport is not required.
  • Application must be submitted by a parent or legal guardian.
  • Original or certified copy of birth certificate / Tazkira is required.
  • Passport will be issued under the consent and responsibility of the parent or guardian.
  • The parent/guardian should be ready to go through a simple verification process (KYC check) to confirm the child’s details.
Documents Required for New Passport Application:

For applicant and dependents 18 years old and above:

  • Taskira (Front and Back)
  • Existing Passport
  • Photograph
  • Residence Country Document

For dependent children less than 18 years old:

  • Taskira (Front and Back)
  • Photograph
  • Residence Country Document
  • Father Passport
  • Mother Passport

For newborn:

  • Taskira (Front and Back)
  • Photograph
  • Residence Country Document
  • Father Passport
  • Mother Passport
  • Birth Certificate

Afghan Overseas Identification (AOID)

Here are the guidelines for applying for Afghan Overseas ID

Eligibility Criteria:

To apply for an AOID, the following criteria must be met:

  1. Proof of Afghan Nationality
    • Tazkira (Afghan National ID) must be provided.
    • Then any of the below:
      • Afghan passport
      • Birth Certificate
      • Or other government-issued documents
  2. Residency Abroad
    • Applicants must reside outside Afghanistan on a long-term or permanent basis.
    • Proof of residency abroad may include:
      • A proof of residency
      • Visa
      • National ID
      • Second citizenship passport
      • Other relevant documents
  3. Minimum Age Requirement to Apply for AOID
    • Applicants must be at least 18 years old to apply independently.
    • Minors may be eligible to apply with the consent of a legal guardian who is also registered in the AOID system.
  4. Biometric Data Submission
    • Applicants must submit biometric data (e.g., liveness check, photograph) as part of the identity verification process.
    • This enhances security and prevents identity fraud.
  5. Consent to Document Verification
    • Applicants must consent to verification of their documents and identification through Afghan government agencies.
    • Where necessary, verification through relevant host-country authorities may also be required.
  6. E-Afghan Portal Account
    • The applicant must first create an e-afghan portal account to access the AOID application form.
  7. Application Fee Payment
    • Applicants must pay an application fee to process the AOID application.
    • Fee waivers may apply in certain cases, such as for students or low-income applicants.

    Receive Your AOID

Birth Certificate

The birth certificate service assists Afghan citizens living abroad in obtaining official proof of birth for legal and personal use. The platform simplifies the process by serving as a bridge between applicants and the Afghan consulate.

Eligibility
  • Afghan citizens residing in other locations serviced by the Afghan consulates.
  • Birth registration can be applied for children born abroad or within Afghanistan.
Required Documents
  • Applicant’s Tazkira (Afghan National ID) or passport
  • Birth registration form (available online through the portal)
  • Parent(s)’ identification (Tazkira, passport, or marriage certificate)
  • Proof of birth (e.g., hospital certificate or similar documentation)
Application Process
  • Register/login to the E-Afghans Portal.
  • Fill out the online birth registration form.
  • Upload all required documents in the prescribed format.
  • Submit the application and pay the necessary fees online.
Processing Time
  • Standard processing time: 4 weeks
  • Applicants will receive updates on their application status via email and they may check real-time status on the portal.
Delivery Options
  • The consulate will notify applicants once the certificate is ready for collection.
  • In some cases, delivery via courier services may be arranged.
Support
  • Live customer support and detailed guidance are available through the E-Afghans Portal.

Frequently Asked Questions

If you need assistance with the Birth Certificate application process, please visit our Helpdesk or contact us at support@eafghans.com for more information.

  • Afghan National ID (Tazkira) or passport copies of both parents.
  • Marriage certificate of the parents (if applicable).
  • Passport-sized photos of the child.
  • Completed Birth Certificate Application Form.

Yes, you are highly encouraged to book an appointment through our online system to ensure timely service and to avoid long waiting times at the consulate.

No, the Afghan Birth Certificate issued by the consulate is not a replacement for a Tazkira (Afghan National ID). The Birth Certificate serves as official proof of birth and Afghan nationality, while the Tazkira is the primary form of identification for Afghan citizens.

Marriage Certificate

The Marriage Certificate Service enables Afghan citizens abroad to register, verify, or request a marriage certificate through the E-Afghans Portal in collaboration with the Afghan consulates. This ensures that marriage documentation is legally recognized and easily accessible.

Eligibility
  • Afghan citizens seeking to register a marriage for the first time or obtain a duplicate/certified copy of an existing marriage certificate.
  • Both parties must present valid identification and proof of marriage.
Required Documents
  • For Registration:
  • Tazkira (Afghan National ID) or passport of both spouses.
  • Marriage contract or agreement (Nikah Nama) in original form.
  • Witness details and IDs (if required).
  • Passport-sized photos of both spouses.
  • For Duplicate/Certified Copy Requests:
  • A copy of the original marriage certificate or related documents (if available).
  • Tazkira or passport of the applicant.
Application Process
  • Step 1: Login/register on the E-Afghans Portal.
  • Step 2: Choose the "Marriage Certificate" service and complete the online form.
  • Step 3: Upload all required documents in the specified format.
  • Step 4: Pay the applicable processing fee online.
  • Step 5: Submit the application and await confirmation from the consulate.
Processing Time
  • Standard processing time: 4 weeks
  • Updates on the status of your application are provided via email and you can check the real-time status in the portal.
Fees
  • The fee structure is displayed during the application process, depending on the type of service (registration, duplicate, or amendments).
Delivery Options
  • Certificates can be collected in person at the consulate.
  • In some cases, courier delivery may be available (additional charges may apply).
Support and Assistance
  • The E-Afghans Portal offers 24/7 support for inquiries and step-by-step guidance to complete the process smoothly.

Frequently Asked Questions

Document Validation/Attestation

The Document Attestation/Validation service allows Afghan citizens abroad to authenticate their official documents for use in Afghanistan or for international purposes. This service is crucial for ensuring that documents such as birth certificates, marriage certificates, academic transcripts, and other official paperwork are recognized by Afghan authorities or foreign governments.

Eligibility
  • Afghan citizens living abroad who need their documents attested for use in Afghanistan or in other countries.
  • The attestation can apply to a wide range of documents, including but not limited to:
    • Birth certificates
    • Marriage certificates
    • Academic degrees and transcripts
    • Commercial documents (e.g., contracts, powers of attorney)
    • Personal identification documents (e.g., passports, Tazkira)
    • Legal documents (e.g., affidavits, court judgments)
Required Documents
  • For Document Attestation:
    • The original document requires attestation.
    • A copy of the applicant’s Tazkira (Afghan National ID) or passport.
    • Any supporting documents related to the document being attested (e.g., if attesting a degree, the transcript might also be required).
  • For Certain Documents (e.g., marriage certificates or legal documents):
    • Additional supporting documents might be required depending on the type of document being attested.
Application Process
  • Step 1: Log in or register on the E-Afghans Portal.
  • Step 2: Select the "Document Attestation" service.
  • Step 3: Choose the type of document to be attested (e.g., academic certificates, legal documents).
  • Step 4: Upload the required documents (original and copies) in the specified format.
  • Step 5: Complete any additional forms (e.g., declaration forms) if required by the consulate.
  • Step 6: Pay the attestation fee (if applicable) and submit the application.
  • Step 7: Wait for the consulate to process and validate your document. You may receive updates on the status of your application through the portal.
Processing Time
  • The processing time for document attestation varies depending on the type of document and consulate workload. Usually, it takes 4-6 weeks to complete the attestation.
  • Applicants will receive notifications regarding the status of their attestation request via email and they may also check the status in the portal.
Delivery and Collection
  • Once attested, the document can be collected in person at the consulate or embassy.
  • Some consulates may offer a document delivery service via courier for an additional fee.
  • Applicants will be notified once the document is ready for collection or has been sent for delivery.
Support and Assistance
  • The portal provides step-by-step instructions for applicants on how to complete the attestation process.
  • Customer support is available to answer questions or guide users through any issues with the document attestation process.

Emergency help for Aghan nationals abroad

The Emergency Assistance service is designed to support Afghan citizens living abroad in urgent or crisis situations. This service is provided through the consulates and is available to Afghan nationals who face emergencies, such as medical issues, natural disasters, accidents, legal problems, or other unforeseen circumstances. The E-Afghans Portal acts as a platform to facilitate communication with consular authorities, ensuring timely and effective help.

Eligibility
  • Afghan citizens currently living abroad who are facing an emergency situation that requires immediate consular assistance.
  • This service is available for both individuals and families in distress, and it can include various types of emergency support.
Types of Emergency Assistance
  • Medical Emergencies:
    • Assistance with medical referrals or coordination with local healthcare providers.
    • Help with emergency medical evacuation or repatriation of deceased citizens.
  • Legal Issues:
    • Support for Afghan citizens who are detained, arrested, or facing legal challenges abroad.
    • Assistance with locating legal representation and communication with local authorities.
  • Natural Disasters or Accidents:
    • Help during natural disasters (e.g., earthquakes, floods) or accidents involving Afghan citizens.
    • Coordination for evacuation, shelter, and relief efforts.
  • Lost or Stolen Documents:
    • Immediate assistance for those who lose their passport, Tazkira, or other essential documents.
    • Help in arranging for emergency travel documents, such as an emergency passport or travel permit.
  • Repatriation Assistance:
    • Help with arranging the return of Afghan citizens to Afghanistan or their home country in the event of a severe crisis.
Application Process
  • Fill out this form: https://eafghans.com/citizen-services/#special-services/emergency-assistance
Processing Time
  • Emergency assistance requests are typically handled on an immediate basis. The consulate aims to respond as quickly as possible depending on the nature of the emergency.
  • For medical or life-threatening emergencies, the response is prioritized.
Fees
  • Depending on the type of emergency assistance, there may be fees associated with certain services (e.g., for repatriation or travel documents).
  • However, many types of emergency assistance (such as urgent communication or medical referrals) may not require additional charges.
Support and Communication
  • For critical emergencies, direct communication with consular staff is available through the portal or by phone.
  • The portal may also provide emergency contact numbers for the nearest Afghan consulate or embassy to further expedite assistance.
Delivery of Assistance
  • Medical: Coordination for medical evacuation or local treatment.
  • Legal: Facilitation in legal cases, including lawyers and consulate representation.
  • Documents: Issuance of temporary travel documents.
  • Repatriation: Arrangements for safe travel back to Afghanistan or the home country.

The E-Afghans Portal provides Afghan citizens abroad with a quick, reliable means of requesting and receiving emergency support from consulates. It simplifies the process of reaching out for help in critical situations, ensuring that Afghan nationals receive the necessary assistance without delays.
