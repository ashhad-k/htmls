New Passport Application

Download Guide Apply for New Passport

Details about applying for a new or renewing an existing passport go here.

Important:

We are currently accepting new passport applications for Newborns only.

Eligibility for New Passport Application

Before proceeding with the new passport application, please check if you are eligible:

  • You are an Afghan citizen.
  • You have not yet applied for a passport.
  • At least one parent must be an Afghan citizen.
  • Both parents (or legal guardians) must submit valid identification documents.
Documents Required for New Passport Application

Documents required for New Passport Application:

  • Completed Passport Application Form
  • Birth Certificate
  • Mother Passport/Taskira
  • Father Passport/Taskira
  • Taskira
  • Passport Photo
New Passport Application Process

Follow the below steps to apply for a new passport

Download Guide Apply for New Passport
  • 1
    Sign In or Create an Account

    Log in to your E-Afghan account or create a new account if you are a first-time user.

  • 2
    Access the Passport Services Section

    Once logged in, navigate to the "Passport Services" section under Citizen Services. Click “Apply Now” under New/Renewal Passport.

  • 3
    Fill Out the New Passport Application Form

    Complete the new passport application form with your correct details.

  • 4
    Upload Required Documents

    Upload clear, scanned copies of all required documents making sure that all are legible to avoid rejection of your application.

  • 5
    Proceed to Payment

    Pay the processing fees through our secure online payment system. You will receive a receipt for your payment, which you can track in your account.

  • 6
    Processing

    Once the payment is successful, you will receive a confirmation email that your application will be added to the queue for processing. Notifications will be sent to you at key stages of the process, including when the application is approved.

  • 7
    Receive Your Passport

    Once approved, your renewed passport will be delivered to your registered mailing address.

Processing Time
The typical processing time for passport renewals is 4-8 weeks, depending on your location and the consulate handling your request. You will be notified of any delays or additional requirements.

Frequently Asked Questions

If you need assistance during the passport application process, please visit our Helpdesk or contact us at support@eafghans.com for more information.

Afghan citizens of all ages are eligible to apply for a new passport. Newborns with at least one Afghan parent are also eligible, provided the necessary documentation is submitted.

Note: We are currently accepting new passport applications via the e-Afghans portal for newborns. If you are applying for a passport not for a newborn, you may visit your nearest consulate to process your passport application.

For newborns, you will need:
  • The newborn’s birth certificate
  • Identification documents of both parents
  • Proof of Afghan citizenship of at least one parent
  • A passport-sized photo of the newborn
  • Completed Passport Application Form

Yes, applications can be started online. The E-Afghans Portal allows you to upload documents, complete forms, and track your application status. Some steps, such as identity verification, may still require an in-person visit.

Processing times vary based on the applicant’s location, document verification requirements, and the consulate's processing capacity. The estimated processing time is 4-8 weeks. If there is any delay or need for additional documents, you will be notified immediately.

If you find an error on your passport, contact the consulate immediately for assistance. Corrections may require the submission of supporting documents and could involve additional processing time.

If you do not have a Tazkira, check with your local Afghan consulate on accepted alternative documents or reissue processes for Afghan citizenship verification.

Currently, there is no option for expedited processing. However, this may be considered in the future to better meet the needs of applicants.

Your passport will be mailed to the current address you have indicated on the application form.

If there are any changes to your mailing address, please contact the consulate right away.

Passport Extension

Download Guide Apply for Passport Extension

Here are the guidelines for applying for Passport Extension

Eligibility for Passport Extension (Sticker)

Before proceeding with the passport extension application, please check if you are eligible:

  • You are an Afghan citizen.
  • Applicants must have a valid or recently expired Afghan passport. Extensions are generally available only for passports that have expired or are nearing expiration.
  • Applicants may need to provide a valid reason for requesting an extension, such as continued residence abroad, pending travel plans, or other personal or professional needs.
  • No Serious Criminal Record.
Documents Required for Passport Extension Application

Documents required for Passport Extension Application:

  • If you are applying for yourself and for dependents who are >18 years old:
    - Existing Passport Copy
    - Passport Photograph
    - Taskira (Front and Back Side)
  • If you are applying for a dependent who is <18 years old:
    Existing Passport Copy
    - Passport Photograph
    - Father Passport/Taskira
    - Mother Passport/Taskira
  • If you are applying for a dependent who is a newborn:
    - Existing Passport Copy
    - Passport Photograph
    - Father Passport/Taskira
    - Mother Passport/Taskira
    - Birth Certificate

Afghan Overseas Identification (AOID)

Here are the guidelines for applying for Afghan Overseas ID

Eligibility Criteria:

To apply for an AOID, the following criteria must be met:

  1. Proof of Afghan Nationality
    • Tazkira (Afghan National ID) must be provided.
    • Then any of the below:
      • Afghan passport
      • Birth Certificate
      • Or other government-issued documents
  2. Residency Abroad
    • Applicants must reside outside Afghanistan on a long-term or permanent basis.
    • Proof of residency abroad may include:
      • A proof of residency
      • Visa
      • National ID
      • Second citizenship passport
      • Other relevant documents
  3. Minimum Age Requirement to Apply for AOID
    • Applicants must be at least 18 years old to apply independently.
    • Minors may be eligible to apply with the consent of a legal guardian who is also registered in the AOID system.
  4. Biometric Data Submission
    • Applicants must submit biometric data (e.g., liveness check, photograph) as part of the identity verification process.
    • This enhances security and prevents identity fraud.
  5. Consent to Document Verification
    • Applicants must consent to verification of their documents and identification through Afghan government agencies.
    • Where necessary, verification through relevant host-country authorities may also be required.
  6. E-Afghan Portal Account
    • The applicant must first create an e-afghan portal account to access the AOID application form.
  7. Application Fee Payment
    • Applicants must pay an application fee to process the AOID application.
    • Fee waivers may apply in certain cases, such as for students or low-income applicants.

The Afghan Overseas Identification (AOID) is an official ID issued to Afghan nationals living abroad, allowing them to access consular services and prove their Afghan identity overseas.

Afghan nationals residing outside Afghanistan on a long-term or permanent basis are eligible to apply. Applicants must provide proof of Afghan nationality and residency abroad.

AOID is exclusively applied through the e-Afghan portal. Simply register an account, log in, and follow the instructions under “Apply for AOID.”

You can track your application status in real time through your account on the e-Afghan portal. Updates on your application’s progress will be visible in your dashboard under the “Service Request Summary” section.

If additional documents are required, you will be notified via the e-Afghan portal and by email. You can log in to the portal to upload the requested documents.

Once approved, you will receive both a digital and physical copy of your AOID. Your digital copy will be instantly available to you on the e-Afghan Portal while the delivery of the physical copy of your AOID will be mailed to you within 4 weeks upon approval.

Yes, the AOID has an expiration date, and renewals will be handled through the e-Afghan portal. Renewal requirements and instructions will be provided in your account prior to expiration.

Birth Certificate

The birth certificate service assists Afghan citizens living abroad in obtaining official proof of birth for legal and personal use. The platform simplifies the process by serving as a bridge between applicants and the Afghan consulate.

Eligibility
  • Afghan citizens residing in other locations serviced by the Afghan consulates.
  • Birth registration can be applied for children born abroad or within Afghanistan.
Required Documents
  • Applicant’s Tazkira (Afghan National ID) or passport
  • Birth registration form (available online through the portal)
  • Parent(s)’ identification (Tazkira, passport, or marriage certificate)
  • Proof of birth (e.g., hospital certificate or similar documentation)
Application Process
  • Register/login to the E-Afghans Portal.
  • Fill out the online birth registration form.
  • Upload all required documents in the prescribed format.
  • Submit the application and pay the necessary fees online.
Processing Time
  • Standard processing time: 4 weeks
  • Applicants will receive updates on their application status via email and they may check real-time status on the portal.
Delivery Options
  • The consulate will notify applicants once the certificate is ready for collection.
  • In some cases, delivery via courier services may be arranged.
Support
  • Live customer support and detailed guidance are available through the E-Afghans Portal.

Marriage Certificate

The Marriage Certificate Service enables Afghan citizens abroad to register, verify, or request a marriage certificate through the E-Afghans Portal in collaboration with the Afghan consulates. This ensures that marriage documentation is legally recognized and easily accessible.

Eligibility
  • Afghan citizens seeking to register a marriage for the first time or obtain a duplicate/certified copy of an existing marriage certificate.
  • Both parties must present valid identification and proof of marriage.
Required Documents
  • For Registration:
  • Tazkira (Afghan National ID) or passport of both spouses.
  • Marriage contract or agreement (Nikah Nama) in original form.
  • Witness details and IDs (if required).
  • Passport-sized photos of both spouses.
  • For Duplicate/Certified Copy Requests:
  • A copy of the original marriage certificate or related documents (if available).
  • Tazkira or passport of the applicant.
Application Process
  • Step 1: Login/register on the E-Afghans Portal.
  • Step 2: Choose the "Marriage Certificate" service and complete the online form.
  • Step 3: Upload all required documents in the specified format.
  • Step 4: Pay the applicable processing fee online.
  • Step 5: Submit the application and await confirmation from the consulate.
Processing Time
  • Standard processing time: 4 weeks
  • Updates on the status of your application are provided via email and you can check the real-time status in the portal.
Fees
  • The fee structure is displayed during the application process, depending on the type of service (registration, duplicate, or amendments).
Delivery Options
  • Certificates can be collected in person at the consulate.
  • In some cases, courier delivery may be available (additional charges may apply).
Support and Assistance
  • The E-Afghans Portal offers 24/7 support for inquiries and step-by-step guidance to complete the process smoothly.
