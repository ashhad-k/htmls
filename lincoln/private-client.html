Family & Matrimonial

Separation or divorcing is not an easy task, but when couples and their lawyers take a less adversarial approach, the entire family benefits. This approach is infact encouraged in many jurisdictions and, where appropriate, we are committed to the practice of collaborative law for resolving family law matters, including divorce, separation, financial and property disputes.

When working collaboratively, the parties involved and their legal representatives agree to resolve all issues of their case without the need of contentious court proceedings. They plan together to prepare an agreement, only applying to court to process an agreed form of divorce petition.

This collaborative approach is often faster and more softer than court proceedings. It opens up channels of communication between the parties, which is vital where there is a continued need to co-parent children. It can also help parties to conclude an agreement which they can live with for the long term.

In some instances, the collaborative process is not an option and in such cases we ensure that our clients have competent representation in whichever way they find themselves litigating.
